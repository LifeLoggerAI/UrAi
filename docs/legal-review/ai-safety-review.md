# AI Safety Review

This is not legal advice. This document prepares AI safety issues for professional review.

## Companion / Narrator Positioning

The Companion and narrator should be framed as symbolic and reflective, not clinical or authoritative.

## Reflection, Not Diagnosis

URAI should use “reflection, not diagnosis” as a boundary. It should not claim to identify, diagnose, treat, or predict medical or psychological conditions.

## No Emergency Support

URAI should not be positioned as emergency support. Counsel and safety reviewers should review appropriate boundaries and escalation language before any sensitive support flows are enabled.

## No Clinical Claims

URAI should not claim clinical validation unless reviewed and approved through the appropriate professional process.

## No Lie Detection Claims

URAI should not claim to detect lies, betrayal, deception, or truthfulness.

## Avoid Over-Personalized Manipulation

Companion messaging should not pressure users, exploit vulnerability, or push emotionally manipulative calls to action.

## User Control and Boundaries

* Sensitive systems off by default
* Passport controls visible
* User controls what opens
* Feedback does not request sensitive details
* Exports are user-controlled

## Review Questions

* What disclaimers are required for AI-generated reflections?
* What content boundaries should apply to Companion responses?
* What is appropriate for public demo mode?
* What should be deferred until additional safety review?
